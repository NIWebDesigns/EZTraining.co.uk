Terms & Conditions

 What if I (or a group booked by an organisation) suddenly can’t attend the course which has been paid for?

If you notify EZ Training early enough, i.e. 7 working days notice, we will book you on to a later available course. If however, you fail to notify us in advance or just don’t turn up on the morning of the course, then you will lose your course fee, as besides registration for the course, order of your exams and course pack, this will have also affected a place on the course for another paying candidate. For a company group which does not arrive for the course, the same applies as detailed above, which has also affected the day/s booked and registered for the course being wasted, when another course for other paying applicants could have been arranged and filled for the same dates.

 Can I pay for my course when I arrive at EZ Training?

No. Due to costs for Registration/Book/Exams, and in case you don’t arrive on the day, the course must be paid for in advance in order to book & guarantee your place. This can be done via the website, or if preferred you can call into our training unit and pay by cash or card, although please email or phone before calling up – just to ensure we are there.

 Is there a course cancellation fee if I give plenty of notice?

Yes, there is still a fee = 2.7%.
We require a minimum of 7 working days notice for any postponements or cancellations, otherwise the full course fee is non-refundable.
However, irrespective of how much notice is given for cancelling a course (even if weeks or months in advance), there will be a 2.7% fee deducted from the refund amount. This is due to PayPal charging EZ Training a 2.7% transaction fee when a booking is made and when any refund is given. Therefore please ensure the course dates you are choosing suit you.
Note: There is no charge however if you just want to postpone your course to a later date (with at least 7 working days notice given).

Frequently Asked Questions

 Does my training course fee also include the actual SIA licence (card)?

No. When you successfully complete your training course with EZ Training, you can then apply for an SIA licence (within 3 years maximum) paying the SIA licence fee. We explain this whole process during the training course.

 How must does the SIA licence cost and how long does it last for?

The SIA licence costs £190 (payable to the SIA) and lasts for 3 years. Renewals cost the same as a new SIA application = £190. The whole process is explained during your course.

 SIA Licensing criteria and Right to Work Status

It is the candidates responsibility that they meet the SIA’s licensing criteria before applying for an SIA licence. Please check the Security Industry Authority (SIA) website for more details.

 English is not my first language. Will this matter?

As required by the SIA and Awarding Body, all course candidates are required to complete a short Basic Level 1 English Literature Test Paper on the first morning of the Security course to help assess if they have at least the basic skills of reading and writing in English language. If the candidate does not accept the minimum pass mark of this Basic Level 1 test (or poor English speaking skills), then they will be unable to proceed with the course and will lose their course fee.

 Can I apply for an SIA licence if I have a Criminal Record?

If you have a criminal record, it does not necessarily mean that you will not get a licence. If in any doubts, then please check “Will I pass the Criminality Checks” (via the SIA website) in order to get an indication (but not a 100% guaranteed answer).

 Can I just do the Door Supervision (or Security Guarding) course without doing the First-Aid?

Only if you have a suitable and valid First-Aid qualification with at least 12 months remaining on it, and you can produce the original certificate (for us to check and photocopy on the first morning of your security course). This can include: Emergency First-Aid at Work (EFAW), First Aid at Work (FAW); First Person on Scene (FPOS); First Response Emergency Care (FREC). A basic online first-aid course (with no classroom theory and practical training involved) will not be suitable. Further details can be found via this link:

https://www.gov.uk/government/news/changes-to-the-training-you-need-for-an-sia-licence

 I have tried renewing my SIA licence, but have been informed by the SIA that I am required to do Top-Up training. Can you advise?

The SIA have been considering this for a while, which is now being introduced from 1st October 2021. Just like a some other training courses, including First-Aid, the Door Supervision and Security Guarding roles now require a Refresher in certain modules, such as Physical Intervention theory & practical, Counter-Terrorism, Suspicious Behaviour, Vulnerable People, etc. and First-Aid (if not already achieved or currently valid). Some details can be found via this link: https://www.gov.uk/government/news/changes-to-the-training-you-need-for-an-sia-licence

 I have already completed my Door Supervision (or Security Guarding) course within the last 3 years. Am I required to do the new course with First-Aid?

No, providing you apply for your SIA licence BEFORE 1st October 2021, i.e. possibly by August 2021, to allow enough time for the processing of your licence. Further details are shown in this link: https://www.gov.uk/government/news/changes-to-the-training-you-need-for-an-sia-licence

 Do the courses involve a lot of work?

Although plenty of guidance and support is given throughout, the courses do involve: Pre-Course Homework Study issued to candidates before their course starts, as well as a number of Theory and Practical scenario sessions throughout the course, to include writing, reading, listening and speaking.

There are a lot of multiple-choice exam questions involved on the last day(s), with the flexible requirement to get at least 75% correct, although with all theory and discussions during the course you will be ready for the exams. We have a 99% pass rate, and in the unlikely event anyone was to fail an exam, we guarantee a FREE resit of the exam.

 Does the training include any physical activity?

Yes, the first-aid day of the training for Door Supervision and Security Guarding includes practical training - giving CPR to a training manikin, which involves giving 30 chest compression's over a period of cycles. Physical activity is also required during the teaching of person-searching demonstrations, scenarios and during the Physical Intervention techniques in the Door Supervision course, which also involves safely escorting a person downstairs and upstairs (with colleagues' help). Please get in touch if you have any queries regarding this, and/or you may want to consult with your doctor if required.

EZ Training’s Complaints and Appeals Procedure

  • Definition of formal complaint
  • Making a formal complaint

A formal complaint is an expression of dissatisfaction concerning EZ Training’s product or service, when the complainant has drawn his or her concern to the attention of one of EZ Training’s employees and is not satisfied with the response.

EZ Training takes all complaints extremely seriously and all staff are trained and committed to rectify any problem as soon as it is brought to their attention. It is recognized that a customer who has a complaint dealt with to their complete satisfaction is likely to become a more loyal customer.

EZ Training, Unit B23, Valley Business Centre, 67 Church Road, Newtownabbey, BT36 7LS. Email: info@eztraining.co.uk. Tel: 0745 4433773.

If you are dissatisfied with the way your problem has been dealt with by a front line member of staff, we encourage you to get in touch via the details shown above. You have the choice as to whether you wish to have your formal complaint dealt with by telephone or by letter, fax or e-mail.

If you prefer to have your complaint dealt with in writing. Please forward details of the complaint to EZ Training, via the full address shown above. You should include as much information as possible, including the nature of the problem, the date the problem occurred and details of who you have spoken to at EZ Training about the problem. You should also tell us what you think we should do to resolve your complaint. Please remember to provide full details of the address where you would like the response to be sent.

Receipt of the complaint will be acknowledged on the same day (or as soon as possible) that it is made by e-mail (where an email address is supplied to EZ Training). Letters will be sent out first class on the day of receipt or as soon as possible.

We undertake to treat the complaint confidentially and to investigate it impartially and thoroughly. A written report will be sent within 10 working days, although every effort will be made to respond in five working days. Details of the investigation and our proposed remedial action will be included within the response.

Details of all complaints will be kept on the complaint file and used to assist staff training and annual performance appraisals.
